# Positioning

## Core Position

TwentyGuard is a strict but calm macOS app that protects your eyes by enforcing
healthy screen breaks, limiting postpones, tracking eye-health rhythm, and
optionally locking the screen at night.

## Primary Audience

- Developers, designers, writers, researchers, and remote workers who spend most
  of the day on a Mac.
- People who already know they should take breaks but keep postponing them.
- Users who prefer a native, local-first utility over a heavy wellness suite.

## Problem

Most break reminders are easy to dismiss. They help users who already have good
habits, but they fail when the real problem is avoidance: one more compile, one
more message, one more video, one more hour at night.

## Product Promise

TwentyGuard creates enough friction for breaks to actually happen, without
turning eye care into a noisy productivity system.

## Differentiation

- Strict screen overlays instead of disposable notifications.
- Postpone limits so breaks cannot be delayed forever.
- Health statistics that explain the problem instead of only showing numbers.
- Optional night screen lock for users who need a real evening boundary.
- Native macOS menu bar experience.
- Local-first architecture.

## Longer Description

TwentyGuard is a native macOS menu bar app for people who keep working through
their eye breaks. It follows the 20-20-20 rule, supports custom work and rest
durations, limits repeated postpones, shows health statistics, and can enforce a
night screen lock when you want a real boundary between work and rest.

## Tone

- Calm, not cute.
- Firm, not punitive.
- Practical, not medicalized.
- Transparent, not growth-hacky.

## Claims To Avoid

- Do not promise to cure eye strain, headaches, insomnia, or vision problems.
- Do not imply clinical validation unless evidence is added.
- Do not describe it as a medical device.
- Do not overstate privacy beyond what the code currently guarantees.
